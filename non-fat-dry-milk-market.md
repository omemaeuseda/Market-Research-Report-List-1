<p><ul><li>Low-Heat</li><li>Medium-Heat</li><li>High-Heat</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The non-fat dry milk market is categorized into three types based on the processing temperature: low-heat, medium-heat, and high-heat. Low-heat non-fat dry milk is produced at lower temperatures, retaining more nutrients, making it suitable for infant formulas and yogurt. Medium-heat non-fat dry milk undergoes moderate processing, ideal for bakery and confectionery products. High-heat non-fat dry milk is processed at elevated temperatures, resulting in greater shelf stability and is commonly used in canned products and processed foods where enhanced solubility is needed.</p></p>
