<p><p>The medical audiological devices market features various hearing aid types tailored to user needs. Behind-the-ear (BTE) aids sit on the outer ear, while receiver-in-the-canal (RITE) units offer a discreet design with a receiver inside the ear canal. In-the-canal (IIC) and completely in-the-canal (CIC) models provide further invisibility for mild to moderate hearing loss. Body-worn hearing aids are larger devices often used for profound loss. Other devices may include bone conduction and implantable aids, catering to diverse audiological requirements.</p></p>
