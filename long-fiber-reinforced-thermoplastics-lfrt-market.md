<p><p>Long Fiber Reinforced Thermoplastics (LFRT) are composite materials that enhance the properties of thermoplastics through the incorporation of long fibers, offering improved strength and durability. In the LFRT market, Polyacrylonitrile (PAN)-based materials utilize PAN fibers, known for their high strength-to-weight ratio and resistance to creep, making them ideal for lightweight applications. Pitch-based LFRTs, on the other hand, employ pitch fibers, which provide excellent thermal and mechanical performance, often used in high-temperature and structural applications. Both types cater to diverse industrial needs, driving growth in automotive, aerospace, and consumer goods sectors.</p></p>
